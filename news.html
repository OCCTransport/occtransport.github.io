Semester Bulletin January 18, 2026

Spring 2026 Semester Bulletin & Schedule Release

Welcome to the Spring 2026 Semester, Bearcats! OCC Transport is happy to be back running full service with some improvements and additions to service for you this semester.

OCCT is proud to introduce the expansion and improvement of its local bus routes, including the Vestal Shopping Shuttle (VS), Downtown Southside (DS), and Main Street (MS), designed to provide more convenient, accessible, and frequent service for our riders.

Formerly the River Ridge Town Square Mall Shuttle (RRT), the route was rebranded last semester as the Vestal Shopping Shuttle (VS), continuing to serve popular destinations such as Walmart and Target while adding new shopping options, including The Shoppes at Vestal. Now, the route has become more accessible with three additional stops along Vestal Avenue: (1) Rail Trail; (2) Willow Point; and (3) Westminster Gardens. The Rail Trail in Vestal is brand new spanning over 2 miles long, just opening last year! Willow Point and Westminster Gardens are living spaces which are now accessible via the VS. These additions provide riders with more shopping, recreational, and residential connectivity than ever before!

The Downtown Southside (DS) route has been greatly improved with more frequent and accessible service. The route has slightly changed; instead of passing through State Street, the DS will now go through Water and Hawley Streets, adding two stops: Henry & Water Sts and Court & Hawley Sts.

The Main Street (MS) route has been reconfigured to improve efficiency, now traveling through Downs Avenue and Glenwood Avenue before and after servicing the Gannett Building. This change provides a quicker, smoother ride, though the Floral, Matthews/Crary, and Crestmont stops will no longer be served. The Helen stop has been moved for both the MS and Westside (WS) routes. Now, the outbound stop for Helen is at the BC Transit sign between KFC and Citizens Bank; the inbound stop is at the electrical pole just before Advance Auto Parts.

OCCT is currently taking applications for our Spring 2 Training class! If you’re 18 or older, have a U.S. driver’s license for at least one year, with no active violations or points, apply to drive the iconic blue buses! Visit occtransport.org/pages/employment for more information.

As we get into the semester, please remember to scan your ID when boarding the bus, and follow all rules of riding the bus. We look forward to another semester of providing you with safe, courteous, and reliable transportation. Best of luck in the semester, and thank you for continuing to be what makes OCCT so great!

For more information regarding the changes noted, please visit occtransport.org, or visit occtransport.org/pages/contactus to ask any questions.

Andrew Monti, Public Relations Coordinator
pr@occtransport.org | 607-777-6916

Semester Bulletin August 8, 2025

Fall 2025 Semester Schedules & Service Bulletin

Welcome to the Fall 2025 Semester, Bearcats! OCC Transport is excited to be back to full service, with some improvements and new additions to make your commute better this semester.

OCCT is proud to introduce its newly extended and improved routes: Vestal Shopping Shuttle (VS), Oakdale Commons Shuttle (OC), and Downtown Southside (DS) Inbound!

Formerly known as the Rivera Ridge Town Square Mall Shuttle (RRT), the RRT has been renamed the Vestal Shopping Shuttle (VS). It now follows the same route as before, including stops at favorites like Target and Walmart, but with an extended route. Additionally, The Shoppes at Vestal and Campus Plaza are now key destinations. The entire route takes 45 minutes, and ALL VS OUTBOUNDS DEPART FROM THE UNION, NOT RAFUSE.

The Oakdale Commons Shuttle (OC) has also been extended. After servicing the Oakdale Commons and Wegmans stops, it continues to stop at Aldi on Harry L Drive and at Five Below. The total trip takes about 35 minutes, with ALL OC OUTBOUNDS DEPARTING FROM THE UNION, NOT RAFUSE.

The demand for bus service in Binghamton’s Southside neighborhood has grown significantly over the past few semesters; however, OCCT was unable to meet that demand earlier due to a labor shortage. When the Downtown Southside (DS) Outbound route was introduced last year, it only operated after 6 P.M. with four runs each night. Now, on weekdays, the DS Outbound starts at 7 A.M., and the newly DS Inbound begins at 8 A.M., totaling 31 DS runs each weekday!

As we begin the semester, please remember to scan your ID when boarding and follow all bus rules and regulations. Drivers will remind passengers to scan IDs over the first few weeks and may deny boarding if an ID cannot be presented.

OCCT is now accepting applications for our Fall 2 Training class! If you’re 18 or older, have had a U.S. driver’s license for at least a year, and have no active violations or points, apply to become a driver of our iconic blue buses! Visit occtransport.org/pages/employment for more details.

We look forward to providing you with another semester of safe, courteous, and reliable transportation. Best of luck this semester, and thank you for making OCCT great!

Andrew Monti, Public Relations Coordinator

Semester Bulletin August 5, 2024

Fall 2024 Semester Schedules & Service Bulletin

Welcome to the Fall 2024 Semester, Bearcats! OCC Transport is happy to be back running full service with some improvements and additions to service for you this semester.

OCCT is proud to introduce a new route, the Downtown Southside (DS)! This route provides service between Main Campus and the UDC via Southside neighborhoods and Downtown Binghamton. This semester, it will only run in the Outbound direction (Campus to UDC) in the evening hours when BC Transit stops operating the 5. As we increase staffing levels and review passenger demand, a mirrored Inbound run will begin operating in the following semesters. Visit occtransport.org/pages/ds for more information!

Since the release of the Downtown Southside, we will no longer be operating the Leroy Southside (LRS). Being the oldest route in our roster, operating since the early 1990s, this route’s service area is currently covered by other routes throughout our service hours and therefore defunct.

Since our staffing levels have decreased from the previous year, we have slightly reduced service and will operate one less bus daily. This amounts to roughly a 9.5% decrease in service. As a result, we have shifted more residential runs (WS, DCL) to direct UDC service to ensure the same or more frequent service to/from the UDC as in previous semesters. We hope to return service frequency to previous numbers in the following semesters as we replenish our staffing levels in upcoming training classes.

OCCT is currently taking applications for our Fall 2 Training class! If you’re 18 or older, had a U.S. driver’s license for at least one year, with no active violations or points, apply to drive the iconic blue buses! Visit occtransport.org/pages/employment for more information.
