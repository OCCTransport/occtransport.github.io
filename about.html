About OCC Transport

We are Binghamton University's student-run transit agency serving the University Community since 1971.

Who We Are

OCC Transport Inc. (OCCT) is a student-run transit organization providing free bus service to the Binghamton University community. Our mission is to provide safe, reliable, convenient, consistent, and courteous transportation for the greater Binghamton University community.

We operate 19 routes with a fleet of 21 vehicles, staffed by 75+ drivers, 3 coordinators, 2 directors, and 4 mechanics. Almost all of our drivers, dispatchers, and coordinators are Binghamton University students, making OCCT one of the largest majority student-run transit agencies in the country.

All buses are free to ride with a valid BU ID. OCCT is funded through the Student Association fee and operates under SA oversight.

History

1971
The First Bus: "Maxwell"

The Off Campus College Council (OC3) purchased a used school bus from Whitney Point Central School District for $800. The bus was named "Maxwell" and painted blue — establishing the signature color carried by every OCCT vehicle since.

1979
Growing to Five Buses

By 1979, the operation had expanded to five buses and multiple routes, connecting more students to off-campus housing across Vestal, Binghamton, and the surrounding area.

1995
Board of Directors Established

A formal Board of Directors was established in 1995, bringing structured governance to the growing organization while maintaining its student-run identity.

Today
19 Routes, 10,000+ Weekday Riders

OCCT now operates 19 routes with a fleet of 21 vehicles, carrying over 10,000 passengers on weekdays. Live bus tracking is available through our partner ETA SPOT.
